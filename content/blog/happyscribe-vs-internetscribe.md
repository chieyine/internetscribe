## The "Real Talk": The Economics of Transcription

Why does HappyScribe charge so much?

To be fair to them, they aren't evil. They have costs. When you upload a file to HappyScribe, they have to:

1.  Store that file on a server (Amazon S3 costs money).
2.  Process that file on a GPU (NVIDIA chips are expensive).
3.  Pay developers to maintain the cloud infrastructure.

Their business model is **Centralized AI**. They do the work on their machines, so they charge you for the rent.

But there is a new model: **Decentralized AI**.

This is what **[InternetScribe](/)** uses. Instead of doing the work on _our_ servers, we give your browser the instructions to do the work on _your_ computer.

Your laptop has a GPU. Your phone has a Neural Engine. These chips are powerful. They are sitting there, mostly idle, while you browse the web.

By using your own hardware to transcribe your own files:

- **We pay $0** for processing.
- **You pay $0** for the service.
- **Your data never leaves your device.**

It’s not magic. It’s efficiency. And it makes the €10/hour fee look ridiculous.

## Deep Dive: Why "Free" Is Actually Faster

One of the biggest myths is that "Paid = Better/Faster."

In the world of AI transcription, the opposite is often true.

### The Speed Bottleneck

When you use a cloud service like HappyScribe:

1.  **Upload:** You have to upload your 500MB video file. On a slow connection, this takes 10 minutes.
2.  **Queue:** Your file sits in a queue waiting for a server to become available.
3.  **Process:** The server processes it (usually at 2x speed).
4.  **Download:** You get the text.

When you use **InternetScribe**:

1.  **Upload:** Instant. There is no upload. The file opens locally.
2.  **Process:** Your computer starts crunching immediately. Modern chips (like Apple M1/M2/M3) are optimized for this. They can often transcribe at **10x to 20x speed**. A 1-hour file can be done in 3 minutes.
3.  **Download:** Instant.

By removing the internet from the equation, we remove the bottleneck.

### The Accuracy Myth

"But surely HappyScribe's AI is smarter?"

Not necessarily. Most transcription services use the same underlying models. Many wrap OpenAI's **Whisper** or similar transformer models.

InternetScribe uses optimized versions of these same models. Because we run locally, we don't have to "dumb down" the model to save server costs. We can let you run a high-quality model because _you_ are providing the compute power.

## The "Hidden" Angle: Privacy is the Killer Feature

We need to talk about your data.

When you use a cloud transcription service, you are handing over your audio. Read their Terms of Service.

- Do they store your audio? Yes.
- Do employees have access to it for "quality assurance"? Often, yes.
- Is it vulnerable to a data breach? Always.

If you are a **lawyer** transcribing client meetings, a **doctor** dictating patient notes, or a **journalist** protecting a source, cloud transcription is a risk.

**Local Transcription is Zero-Knowledge.**
With InternetScribe, the code runs in your browser sandbox. We literally _cannot_ see your audio or your transcript. We don't have a database of your files. If the FBI knocked on our door and asked for your transcripts, we would have nothing to give them.

That peace of mind is worth more than €10/hour. It’s priceless.
