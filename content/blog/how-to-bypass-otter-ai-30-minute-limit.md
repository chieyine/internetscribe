## Method 1: Split Long Audio Files into Chunks

**Time required:** 10-20 minutes  
**Success rate:** 100%  
**Risk:** None

The simplest workaround: cut your long audio into sub-40-minute segments before uploading.

### How to Split Audio Files

**Option A: Using Audacity (Free)**

1. Download [Audacity](https://www.audacityteam.org/)
2. Open your audio file
3. Select first 35 minutes (leave 5-min buffer)
4. File > Export > Export as MP3
5. Name it "Meeting-Part1.mp3"
6. Repeat for remaining segments

**Option B: Using Online Tools**

1. Go to mp3cut.net or audio-cutter.com
2. Upload your file
3. Set start/end times for 35-minute chunk
4. Download segment
5. Repeat for remaining parts

**Option C: Using FFmpeg (Command Line)**

For technical users, split a 2-hour file into 35-minute chunks:

```bash
ffmpeg -i meeting.mp3 -f segment -segment_time 2100 -c copy meeting_part%d.mp3
```

### The Downside

- Time-consuming for multiple files
- Transcripts come in separate pieces
- Context may be lost at split points
- Still subject to 300-minute monthly cap

## Method 2: The Monthly Reset Wait

**Time required:** Up to 30 days  
**Success rate:** 100%  
**Risk:** None

If you've hit your 300-minute monthly cap but don't have urgent needs, simply wait for it to reset.

### How Otter's Billing Cycle Works

Your 300 minutes reset on your account anniversary date, not the 1st of the month. To check:

1. Go to Otter.ai
2. Click your profile → Settings
3. View "Usage" or "Subscription" section
4. Note your reset date

### Optimizing Around Reset Dates

If you know your reset date, schedule transcription-heavy work accordingly:

- Save heavy transcription for just after reset
- Use remaining minutes at end of cycle
- Space out usage to avoid mid-month shortages

### The Downside

- Unpredictable work gets delayed
- Doesn't solve urgent transcription needs
- Only helps with monthly cap, not 40-minute limit
