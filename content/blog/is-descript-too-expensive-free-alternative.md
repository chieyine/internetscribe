## What Makes Descript Expensive (And Worth It for Some)

Before comparing alternatives, let's understand what you're actually paying for with Descript:

### Features That Justify the Price

1. **Edit Audio by Editing Text** — Descript's killer feature lets you delete words from the transcript to cut them from the audio
2. **Overdub (AI Voice Cloning)** — Record corrections without re-recording
3. **Screen Recording** — Built-in recording with transcription
4. **Multi-track Editing** — Full audio/video editing timeline
5. **Filler Word Removal** — Automatically cut "um," "uh," and other filler words
6. **Studio Sound** — AI-powered audio enhancement

### What You're Paying For (That You Might Not Need)

If your workflow is:

1. Record audio → 2. Get transcript → 3. Export and use elsewhere

Then you're paying $15–44/month for features you'll never touch. The transcript is just 10% of what Descript offers, but for many users, it's 100% of what they need.

Not with InternetScribe—that's Descript's unique feature. However, you can export timestamped transcripts to use with traditional audio editors like Audacity or Adobe Audition.

InternetScribe requires an internet connection for AI processing. If you need offline transcription, check out our guide to [OpenAI Whisper](/blog/how-to-use-gemini-flash-for-transcription) for local processing.
